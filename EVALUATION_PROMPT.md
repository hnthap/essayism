# Universal Essay Evaluation Framework

## Core Dimensions (Adaptable by Context)

### 1. **Thesis and Focus** (0-20 points)

- Is there a clear, specific central claim or purpose?
- Does the essay maintain focus on this thesis throughout?
- Is the thesis appropriately scoped (not too broad, not too narrow)?
- Does the thesis demonstrate original or critical thinking?

**Variations:** Personal narratives may have an implicit thesis about meaning/significance rather than an explicit argument. Exploratory essays may pose questions rather than answer them.

---

### 2. **Argumentation and Logic** (0-25 points)
- Are claims supported with reasoning?
- Is the logic sound (free from fallacies)?
- Are counterarguments acknowledged and addressed?
- Do conclusions follow from premises?
- Is the chain of reasoning clear and coherent?

**Variations:** This matters most in argumentative/persuasive essays. In descriptive or narrative essays, this becomes "coherence of perspective" or "meaningful reflection."

---

### 3. **Evidence and Support** (0-20 points)
- Are claims backed by specific examples, data, quotations, or analysis?
- Is evidence relevant and sufficient?
- Is evidence from credible sources (when applicable)?
- Is evidence integrated smoothly rather than dropped in?
- Does the writer analyze evidence rather than just present it?

**Variations:** Literary analysis requires textual evidence; personal essays require vivid detail and sensory information; scientific essays require empirical data.

---

### 4. **Organization and Structure** (0-15 points)
- Does the essay have a clear introduction, body, and conclusion?
- Do paragraphs follow logically from one another?
- Are transitions effective?
- Does each paragraph have a clear purpose?
- Is there a sense of progression or development?

**Variations:** Creative essays may use non-linear structures effectively. The key is *intentional* structure, not necessarily traditional structure.

---

### 5. **Clarity and Style** (0-15 points)
- Is the writing clear and understandable?
- Are sentences varied and well-constructed?
- Is word choice precise and appropriate?
- Does the writing have a consistent voice/tone?
- Is the style appropriate for the audience and purpose?

**Variations:** Academic essays favor formal precision; personal essays may use conversational tone; literary essays might employ more elaborate prose.

---

### 6. **Engagement and Originality** (0-5 points)
- Does the essay offer fresh insights or perspectives?
- Is it intellectually engaging?
- Does it go beyond obvious or surface-level observations?
- Does it show genuine thinking rather than regurgitation?

**Variations:** Heavily emphasized in advanced academic work; less critical in informative writing.

---

## **Total: 100 points**

---

## Contextual Adjustments

**For argumentative essays:** Weight argumentation (30%) and evidence (25%) most heavily.

**For analytical essays:** Emphasize evidence (30%) and originality of interpretation (20%).

**For personal/narrative essays:** Prioritize clarity/style (25%), specific detail (evidence = 25%), and meaningful reflection (thesis = 25%).

**For research papers:** Evidence quality and source integration become paramount (35%).
